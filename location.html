Location

Cape Town, South Africa

Photos courtesy of Jim Sher
What to do here

A top tourist destination, there are many things to do and see. The Cape Town tourism department has a great website, capetown.travel with some ideas to set you going in the right direction.

If you're an outdoors person, Cape Town has a number of reserves where you can go walking, running, cycling, climbing and more. There are some amazing hikes that take you up Table Mountain or Lion's Head where you can take in the spectacular views of Cape Town.

If you want to spend some time relaxing, there are numerous beaches (although the water is a little chilly), and during the summer months (October-March), the temperature stays between 20-35C (68-95F). There is also Kirstenbosch Gardens, a stunning botanical garden backed against Table Mountain reserve, which is a great place to take in nature, have a picnic, go for a walk, or just relax in the shade.

If you're into surfing or bodyboarding, Cape Town has some sweet surf spots, as well as some huge swells where people from around the world come for some big wave surfing.

Getting Here

Cape Town has an international airport and is easily accessible from anywhere in the world. It has a top-class airport with flights arriving domestically and internationally. To compare the prices of tickets, visit Travelstart.

Airlines flying domestically to Cape Town from within South Africa are: South African Airways, Mango, 1time, Kulula.com. You can also use Travelstart to search across airlines.

Airlines flying internationally to Cape Town include most major international airlines: British Airways, Royal Dutch Airlines, Delta Airways, Lufthansa Airways, Qatar Airways, Emirates

Transport in Cape Town

South Africa has a good long-distance bus system, but using airlines to travel between major cities is recommended due to the time and distance (and better quality of travel). If you do want to go the more scenic route, you can try one of the many bus companies: Greyhound, Translux and Intercape. If you'd prefer the backpacker style of getting around the country, you might want to check out Bazbus.

Once in Cape Town, you can get around using the relatively new MyCiti bus system, or you can use one of the many taxi companies. If you are going to use taxis, we recommend Excite Taxis, or Rikkis.
