TRIPADVISOR #1 VOTED DESTINATION

Kirstenbosch

Cape Town, South Africa

The Venue - Kirstenbosch Botanical Gardens


View My Saved Places in a larger map

Set against the eastern slopes of Table Mountain, Kirstenbosch National Botanical Gardens was established nearly 100 years ago to conserve and promote the diverse indigenous flora of South Africa. Kirstenbosch covers 26 hectares, and falls within the Cape Floristic Region, declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2004. With over 7 000 plant species, interspersed with rolling green lawns, the Garden makes for beautiful and relaxed setting, just 15 minutes from Cape Town's CBD.

Transport in Cape Town

South Africa has a good long-distance bus system, but using airlines to travel between major cities is recommended due to the time and distance (and better quality of travel). If you do want to go the more scenic route, you can try one of the many bus companies: Greyhound, Translux and Intercape. If you'd prefer the backpacker style of getting around the country, you might want to check out Bazbus.

Once in Cape Town, you can get around using the relatively new MyCiti bus system, or you can use one of the many taxi companies. If you are going to use taxis, we recommend Excite Taxis, or Rikkis.

You can rent a car from around R330 (USD 40) per day. There are a number of car rental companies to choose from, but we would suggest you contact Pangea for competitive rates with minimum excess and unlimited mileage.


What to do here

A top tourist destination, there are many things to do and see. The Cape Town tourism department has a great website, capetown.travel with some ideas to get you going in the right direction.

If you're an outdoors person, Cape Town has a number of reserves where you can go walking, running, cycling, climbing and more. There are some amazing hikes that take you up Table Mountain or Lion's Head where you can take in the spectacular views of Cape Town.

If you want to spend some time relaxing, there are numerous beaches (although the water is a little chilly), and during the summer months (October-March), the temperature stays between 20-35C (68-95F). If you're into surfing or bodyboarding, Cape Town has some sweet surf spots, as well as some huge swells where people from around the world come for some big wave surfing.

And if you'd like to explore the local culture and heritage, there's Robben Island (where Nelson Mandela was imprisoned), the Cape Town Castle, Slave Lodge, District Six Museum, the old Cape Malay Quarter in Bokaap, township tours, and a number of restaurants serving traditional cuisine. Just outside of Cape Town, you could travel the Wine Route, tasting wine among picturesque vineyards and farmsteads, some of which date to the 17th century. Or head up to the Cederberg mountain range, where'll you find numerous rock paintings by South Africa's “First Peoples”, the Khoe and San hunter-gatherers.
